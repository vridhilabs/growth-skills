# Write Cold Email

Write a hyper-personalized cold email for a prospect. Give me their name, title, company, and one specific thing about them (a recent post, job listing, company news, or something from their website). Tell me who you are and what you want (demo, intro, partnership, job).

## What to provide

- Prospect: name, title, company
- One hook: something specific about them (recent hire, product launch, content they posted, company news)
- Your context: who you are + what you offer in 1–2 sentences
- Goal: demo / intro / partnership / job

## What you'll get back

Three email variations using different angles:

1. **Pain** — Lead with their specific challenge
2. **Curiosity** — Open a loop, make them want to know more  
3. **Proof** — Lead with a result for someone like them

Plus a Day 3 and Day 7 follow-up.

## Rules I apply

- Subject: max 7 words, no clickbait, no "Quick question"
- Body: max 4 sentences. Never "I hope this finds you well."
- CTA: one specific ask. Never "let me know if you're interested."
- No generic openers. Every email leads with something specific to them.

## Example

**Input:**
> Prospect: Rahul Sharma, Head of Marketing, HealthStack (Series B SaaS). Hook: they just hired 3 SDRs and posted about scaling outbound. My context: I build AI outreach systems that cut SDR ramp time by 40%. Goal: demo.

**Output:**

*Pain angle:*
> Subject: SDR ramp at HealthStack
>
> Rahul — scaling from 0 to 3 SDRs usually means 90 days before anyone's productive. I build AI systems that cut that to 45. Happy to show you what that looks like for HealthStack's motion. 15 minutes this week?

*Follow-up (Day 3):*
> Still thinking about the SDR ramp problem — worth a quick chat?
