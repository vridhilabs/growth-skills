# Competitor Map

Map a competitor's GTM from their public footprint. Give me a competitor name or URL and I'll extract their positioning, growth channels, hiring signals, content themes, and gaps — plus a counter-messaging brief.

## What to provide

- Competitor name or URL
- Your product (optional — for gap analysis and battle card)
- Depth: quick (homepage + pricing) or full (+ careers + blog + ads)

## What you'll get back

**Positioning map:**
- Their core claim and who they're targeting
- What features/benefits they emphasize
- Pricing model signals

**Growth channels** — what they're investing in (inferred from content, ads, job posts)

**Hiring signals** — open roles → what they reveal about the next 6 months
> e.g. "Hiring 3 SDRs → scaling outbound. Hiring ML engineer → building AI features Q3."

**Content themes** — what topics they own and what angle they take

**Gap analysis:**
- Where they're weak
- Where the opening is for you

**Counter-messaging brief:**
- Your positioning angle against them
- 5 battle card bullets for sales

## Example

**Input:**
> Competitor: HubSpot. My product: Twenty CRM (open-source, self-hosted).

**Output:**

**Their claim:** "The easy-to-use CRM." Targets SMB, emphasizes all-in-one suite and integrations.

**Gap:** HubSpot's pricing scales aggressively. Data lives in their cloud. No self-hosted option. Overkill for lean teams.

**Your angle:** Twenty CRM — your data, your server, no per-seat pricing. Built for operators who want control, not a SaaS subscription that grows faster than your revenue.

**Battle card:**
- HubSpot starts free, then hits you at $800/mo for the features you actually need
- Your data is in their cloud. Compliance headache waiting to happen.
- Twenty is MIT-licensed. Fork it, extend it, own it.
- No per-seat pricing. Scale your team, not your CRM bill.
- Built for operators who know what they're doing, not onboarding wizards
