# Content Engine

Turn one signal or idea into platform-native content for LinkedIn, X (Twitter), and Email. Give me a topic, URL, insight, or raw thought — I'll produce content natively formatted for each platform in your voice.

## What to provide

- Signal: a topic, URL, raw idea, or insight (one sentence is enough)
- Persona: founder / operator / analyst / builder
- Audience: who you're writing for
- Platforms: linkedin / x / email (or all three)

## What you'll get back

**LinkedIn post** — 3–5 short paragraphs, line breaks, pattern-interrupt opener, max 2 relevant tags, first comment with supporting detail

**X thread** — Hook tweet + 4 insight tweets + CTA tweet (or a single punchy standalone tweet)

**Email snippet** — Subject line + preview text + 3-paragraph body (problem → insight → CTA)

## Voice modes

| Persona | Style |
|---|---|
| **Founder** | Contrarian, direct, data-backed. Share what works, not theory. |
| **Operator** | Systems-first, practical. Every post has a framework or process. |
| **Analyst** | Metrics-driven, precise. Back every claim with data. |
| **Builder** | Curious, technical. Show don't tell. Share what you're making. |

## Compression rules applied to everything

1. Drop the period — bare endings get ~20% more engagement
2. Land on a noun — last word is a thing, not an action
3. Colons as pivots — keep both sides short (2–4 words)
4. No clutter — no "I'm excited to share," no tribe-signaling, no diary entries

## Example

**Input:**
> Signal: Most growth teams confuse activity with traction. Persona: operator. Audience: startup founders. Platforms: linkedin, x.

**LinkedIn:**
> Most growth teams are very busy going nowhere.
>
> They track impressions. Opens. Clicks. Sessions. All the vanity metrics that feel like momentum but aren't.
>
> Real traction has one signal: are more people paying you this month than last?
>
> Everything else is noise. Including most of your dashboard.
>
> Build the one metric that matters. Then build systems to move it.

**X:**
> most growth teams confuse activity with traction
>
> the difference: one fills reports. one fills your bank account
